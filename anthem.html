A Threshold into Quiet Living

There’s only one way in, and only one way out. That’s not a limitation — it’s a choice. The entrance itself feels like a threshold, a quiet separation from the busier parts of Kyle. Once you’re inside, the pace shifts. The streets grow still, the noise fades, and the neighborhood settles into its own calm.

Living westward means errands take a drive — ten, maybe fifteen minutes — but that distance isn’t a burden. It’s a buffer. It’s the difference between convenience and comfort, between being surrounded by fast-food chains and corner stores, or choosing a setting where the absence of clutter becomes part of the value.

Here, peace is the amenity. The quiet isn’t accidental; it’s the lifestyle. Neighbors know the trade-off, and they embrace it. The separation creates a sense of exclusivity, a community defined not by what’s nearby, but by what’s intentionally kept at arm’s length.

Information to Consider

  • HOA dues are approximately $840 annually (about $210 quarterly).
  • Estimated tax rate is around 2.75%. Without exemptions, on a $450,000 home, annual taxes are roughly $12,375.
  • Anthem is also part of a Municipal Utility District (MUD). The MUD adds an additional tax rate each year to fund water, sewer, drainage, and road infrastructure for the community. This is why Anthem’s overall property tax rate is higher than many nearby neighborhoods that do not have a MUD.
  • Anthem is located in the Kyle ETJ and uses City of Kyle water, which means it was included in the 2025 ETJ water and wastewater rate increase. Because Anthem is outside city limits, ETJ utility rates are higher than in-city rates, and the 2025 adjustment resulted in a significant increase for ETJ customers.
  • Homes are relatively newer, which generally supports a higher likelihood of FHA loan approval.
